Welcome

Life can be overwhelming at times. You don’t have to face it alone.

WWhether you’re navigating anxiety, depression, trauma, relationship challenges, life transitions, or simply feeling stuck, therapy can be a place where you feel comfortable showing up exactly as you are.

Approach

Compassionate, collaborative, strengths-based.

I believe healing looks different for everyone. My work is grounded in meeting you where you are, identifying your strengths, and walking alongside you as you move toward the life you want to live.

  1. i

    Compassionate

    Therapy should be a place where you feel heard, understood, and free from judgment.

  2. ii

    Collaborative

    Together we’ll explore the challenges you’re facing and develop practical tools you can use.

  3. iii

    Strengths-based

    Healing is built on what’s already strong in you. We start there.

My goal is to create a welcoming and supportive space where you feel heard, understood, and empowered. Together, we’ll explore the challenges you’re facing, identify your strengths, and develop practical tools to help you move toward the life you want to live.

Questions

Common questions.

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

There’s no perfect time or perfect reason to start. If you’re feeling overwhelmed, stuck, or simply wanting more from your life, that’s enough. Starting therapy takes courage — and you don’t have to have it all figured out before you reach out.

What can I expect from a first session?

Our first session is a conversation. I’ll ask about what’s bringing you in, what you’re hoping for, and what’s worked or hasn’t worked before. You can ask me anything too. There’s no pressure to have everything figured out.

How long does therapy usually last?

It depends. Some people come in for a few months around a specific challenge; others stay longer to do deeper work. We’ll check in regularly about what feels right for you.
